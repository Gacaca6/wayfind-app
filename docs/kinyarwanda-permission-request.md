# Kinyarwanda text — permission request (draft)

**Status:** the Kinyarwanda text currently bundled in Wayfind is the **BIR — Bibiliya Yera, © Bible Society of Rwanda (2001)**, obtained via the Beblia public XML repository. Beblia re-hosts it with a blanket "use freely" note, but Beblia is **not** the rights holder, so that note is not a valid license. This text is included at the project owner's informed decision for a free, non-commercial Scripture app. To put it on a proper footing, send the request below to the Bible Society of Rwanda and keep their written reply on file.

**Who to contact:**
- Bible Society of Rwanda — https://biblesociety-rwanda.org/ (and via the United Bible Societies / Biblica permissions desks)

---

## Draft email / letter

Subject: Permission request — bundling Bibiliya Yera in a free, non-commercial Bible app (Wayfind)

Dear Bible Society of Rwanda,

My name is [NAME], and I am building **Wayfind**, a free, non-commercial mobile/web app that helps people — especially Rwandans — find and read Scripture by the emotion they are feeling, and read the whole Bible offline. The app contains no advertising, charges no fees, and sells nothing.

I would like to request written permission to include the **Kinyarwanda Bibiliya Yera (BIR, 2001)** text within the app, stored offline on the user's device, displayed alongside the English (public-domain World English Bible). Full copyright and source attribution would be shown in the app (e.g. "Bibiliya Yera © Bible Society of Rwanda"), and I will honor any conditions, attribution wording, verse-limit, or watermarking you require.

If a full-text license is not possible, I would be grateful for guidance on what use you can permit (for example, a maximum number of verses, or an approved API/link-out arrangement), so that Rwandans can read God's Word in their own language within this free app.

Thank you for your ministry and for considering this request. I would be honored to comply fully with your terms.

With gratitude,
